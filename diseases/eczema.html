Severe Eczema: When Symptoms Are Persistent and Severe

What is Severe Eczema?

Eczema, or atopic dermatitis, is a chronic skin condition characterized by inflammation, redness, and itching. Severe eczema refers to a more intense form of eczema, where symptoms become persistent and significantly affect daily life.

Signs and Symptoms of Severe Eczema

When eczema becomes severe, the following symptoms may occur:

  • Intense itching, which may lead to scratching and further irritation.
  • Dry, cracked skin that may bleed or become infected.
  • Thickened, leathery skin due to frequent scratching and inflammation.
  • Redness and swelling in affected areas.
  • Blisters and oozing in some cases, indicating a more severe flare-up.

When Should You Seek Medical Help?

If your eczema symptoms are persistent and severe, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional. Severe eczema may require medical treatments such as:

  • Topical corticosteroids or other prescription creams.
  • Antihistamines to manage itching.
  • Immunosuppressants to control the inflammatory response.
  • Phototherapy in some cases.

Ignoring severe eczema can lead to complications, such as skin infections, scarring, or more frequent flare-ups. Proper treatment is necessary to manage the condition effectively.
